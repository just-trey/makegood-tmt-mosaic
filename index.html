The five-minute version

Pick a part shape, drop in an SVG, and each flat fill color becomes its own recessed region. Export a 3MF and it's pre-mapped to AMS filament slots — open it in Bambu Studio, OrcaSlicer, or Snapmaker Orca and print.

Everything below matches a section in the left panel, in the same order.

Left panel · Part

Choosing a part

The dropdown lists real MakeGood TMT parts — pick one (Wheel, Footrest, …) and its actual mesh loads, with pockets cut directly into it. Disc (reference) is a plain flat-plate insert sized by hand, kept as a quick reference shape.

Body / blank color is the fallback color of the physical blank (neutral grey or a plain filament) — it's what the body prints when no artwork color is grouped into the base. To print an artwork color as the body instead, group it into the Base row in Colors detected below (see "→ base" there) — an artwork base always wins over this fallback.

Download design template gives you that part's design face as a true-to-size (1:1 mm) SVG — its real printable outline, with any no-print gaps already punched out. Draw over it in your editor and load the result under Artwork.

Left panel · Artwork

Preparing your SVG

Use flat, distinct fill colors — no gradients or pattern fills, those get flagged and skipped. Each unique fill becomes a candidate color region before you even touch the fit controls.

For an assembly part, design over the template you downloaded in the Part section above so the size is right. If an editor drops the document's millimeter size on export (Affinity writes width="100%"), the app scales the document's viewBox to fit the part face — setting the editor document to millimeters keeps it exact.

Left panel · Artwork fit

Positioning artwork on the part

Margin sets the auto-fit border. Scale multiplies on top of that — over 100% will bleed past the part's edge. Flip H mirrors left-to-right, useful when text reads backwards after import.

Left panel · Depth

How deep recesses cut

Depth is measured down from the top face. Set a default for all colors, then override individual ones in Colors detected below for fine detail work. Check "Recess bg too" to also cut the background instead of leaving it flush.

Left panel · Colors detected

Merging into AMS slots

The Auto-merge slider collapses visually similar colors automatically — None turns it off, Slight (the default) dedupes near-identical export artifacts, Medium and Strong collapse shading ramps toward fewer filaments. It's fully reversible: drag it back down and colors pull back apart. Click a merged color's "×" to pull just that one out without affecting the rest of the group.

You can also merge manually: drag one color onto another to combine them into a single AMS slot sharing one depth. A merged slot prints in its dominant member's real color, not a blended average. To split a group back up, click its members' "×" swatches (each pulled color stays out until you re-merge it).

The Base row pinned at the top is a slot too — one that prints as the body instead of being cut as a recess. "→ base" on a color (or a whole merged group) makes it the base, swapping out whatever was there; to combine several colors into the base instead, drag them onto the Base row. It shows every color you've grouped in, and it always prints its dominant member's color. Click a base color's "×" to send it back to being cut. Empty the base entirely and the body falls back to the plain Body / blank color picker up in Part.

Left panel · Export

Getting a printable file

Pick your printer, then export. The 3MF is print-ready for Bambu Studio, OrcaSlicer, or Snapmaker Orca with colors pre-assigned to filament slots. The STL set is a per-color .zip fallback for slicers that don't read pre-mapped 3MF color data.
